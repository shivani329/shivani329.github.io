Save the Elephants!

But why?

  • 1. Elephants are beautiful creatures
  • 2. Sadly, they are numbered.
  • 3. Between the years of 2010 and 2012, 100,000 elephants were killed for their ivory tucks in Africa.

Aren't there already plenty of organizations for this?

Yep! In fact, there is already an organization called Save the Elephants that was created to do just that. I'm just making this page for an assignment, and I love that
organization! They sell alot of clothing, raise awareness, have many volunteer or donation opportunities available, and put a lot of research toward how elephants
are doing and what they can do to help. They spread awareness and aim to protect the elephants of the world, however they mostly focus on those in Africa.

What about the other elephants of the world?

Good call! It is important to note that Indian elephants and "domesticated" elephants, like those used in circuses, are just as endangered and at risk as African elephants.
Don't fret, there are too, plenty organizations set up to protect them as well. But, if you are curious, this table shows some of the differences between African and Indian
elephants.
